## 2026-06-20

### Topic: Environment

**El Niño Is Back — Expect a Hotter Year**

- The UN's weather agency (the **WMO**) has confirmed that **El Niño has returned** and is likely to strengthen through the second half of 2026, with forecasters putting the odds of it lasting into November at **90% or higher**.
- *El Niño* is a natural climate pattern in which the central and eastern Pacific Ocean warms up more than usual. This shifts weather worldwide — typically pushing **global temperatures higher** and bringing more extreme weather, such as heatwaves in some regions and unusual droughts or heavy rain in others.
- Most forecast models expect at least a **moderate, possibly strong** event, though how intense it gets is still uncertain.
- The UN urged countries to strengthen **early-warning systems**, cautioning that temperatures will run above average "nearly everywhere." Combined with long-term warming, this makes it "virtually certain" 2026 will land among the **10 hottest years on record**.

**Why it matters:** El Niño sits on top of human-driven warming, so its return raises the risk of record heat, crop stress, and weather disasters — a reminder for vulnerable regions to prepare now.

*Source: WMO / UN News / NOAA, June 2026*

## 2026-06-19

### Topic: Geopolitics

**Ethiopia's Ruling Party Heads for Landslide**

- Ethiopia, Africa's second-most populous country, held its **7th general election on June 1, 2026**, with more than **54 million registered voters** — one of the continent's largest democratic exercises this year.
- Early and verified vote counts show Prime Minister **Abiy Ahmed's Prosperity Party** far in front and on track for a sweeping win, echoing its landslide in 2021. Final nationwide results are being confirmed in mid-June.
- A general election is how voters choose members of parliament; the party that wins the most seats forms the government and its leader stays prime minister. A near-total win means little organised opposition to check the ruling party.
- The vote was **incomplete**: no voting took place in the entire **Tigray region** — still recovering from a recent war — and in parts of Amhara and Oromia, due to ongoing insecurity. Critics say this, plus a weak opposition, limits how representative the result is.

**Why it matters:** A landslide further cements Abiy's grip on a strategically important nation in the Horn of Africa, while unvoted conflict zones are a reminder that Ethiopia's internal divisions remain unresolved.

*Source: Bloomberg / Al Jazeera / Ethiopia's NEBE, June 2026*

## 2026-06-18

### Topic: AI Technology

**Microsoft Builds Its Own AI Models**

- Microsoft unveiled a family of **seven new in-house AI models** at its Build 2026 event, marking its clearest step yet toward relying less on its partner **OpenAI** (the maker of ChatGPT) for core AI.
- Two stand out. **MAI-Thinking-1** is Microsoft's first *reasoning model* — one built to work through problems step by step — scoring strongly on hard maths and software tests. **MAI-Code-1-Flash** is a fast coding assistant that turns plain-English descriptions into working code.
- A notable claim: both were trained *from scratch* on licensed, enterprise-grade data, **without copying** from rivals' models like OpenAI's GPT series — addressing concerns about how AI is trained.
- The goal is cost and independence: cheaper models Microsoft fully controls let it compete directly with OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google across more of the AI stack.

**Why it matters:** It signals the AI market is maturing into rivalry even among close partners, which tends to mean more choice and lower prices for the developers and businesses that use these tools.

*Source: CNBC / Microsoft AI / TechTimes, June 2, 2026*

## 2026-06-17

### Topic: Economics

**Fed Holds Rates as Inflation Lingers**

- The US Federal Reserve — the country's central bank, which sets the cost of borrowing — was widely expected to **keep its key interest rate unchanged** at its meeting concluding today, leaving the target range at **3.50%–3.75%**.
- The interest rate is the main lever the Fed uses to steer the economy: higher rates make loans pricier and cool spending, helping tame *inflation* (the rate at which prices rise); lower rates do the opposite.
- Rates have sat unchanged since December 2025 because inflation is still running **above the Fed's 2% goal**, hovering near three-year highs, partly due to high energy prices tied to the Middle East conflict.
- This is the **first meeting led by new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh**, sworn in last month. Markets are watching his projections for whether a rate cut is off the table — or even a future hike on it.

**Why it matters:** The Fed's rate shapes what you pay on mortgages, car loans, and credit cards, so a continued hold means borrowing stays expensive while the bank waits for inflation to ease.

*Source: CNBC / Kiplinger, June 17, 2026*

## 2026-06-16

### Topic: Environment

**AI's Hidden Power and Water Bill**

- A new United Nations University report measured the environmental footprint of AI. It found the world's **data centres** — the warehouses of computers that run AI services — used about **448 terawatt-hours of electricity in 2025**. If they were a country, that would make them the world's 11th-largest electricity user.
- A *terawatt-hour* is a billion kilowatt-hours; a typical home uses a few thousand kilowatt-hours a year. So this is an enormous amount of power, and AI accounts for about a fifth of it.
- Demand is climbing fast. The report projects data centres could reach **945 terawatt-hours by 2030** — more than double — driven largely by AI growth.
- It's not just electricity: cooling all those machines also consumes huge volumes of **water**, with usage projected to hit trillions of litres by 2030.

**Why it matters:** The AI tools many of us now use daily carry a real-world energy and water cost, raising pressure to power data centres with cleaner electricity and cool them more efficiently.

*Source: UN University (UNU-INWEH) report, via Business Standard / Earth.Org, June 2026*

## 2026-06-15

### Topic: Geopolitics

**US and Iran Strike a Peace Deal**

- The United States and Iran announced they have reached an agreement to end their war, with both sides declaring an immediate and permanent halt to military operations on all fronts, including in Lebanon. An official signing ceremony is set for **Switzerland on Friday, June 19**.
- A headline term: the **Strait of Hormuz will reopen** once the deal is signed. This narrow waterway between Iran and Oman is one of the world's most important oil routes — roughly a fifth of global oil passes through it — so its closure during the war squeezed energy supplies.
- The path here was bumpy. Earlier in June, Iran briefly **suspended talks** in protest over Israel's actions in Lebanon before negotiations got back on track.
- One sticking point remains: Iran's nuclear program. Tehran says 60-day nuclear talks will only begin once the US **releases billions in frozen Iranian funds** — a claim a US official rejected.

**Why it matters:** Reopening the Strait of Hormuz could ease global oil prices and inflation, while the unresolved nuclear question signals the peace is fragile and far from final.

*Source: CNN / Al Jazeera, June 14, 2026*

## 2026-06-14

### Topic: AI Technology

**Gemini 3.1 Ultra Stretches AI's Memory**

- Google released Gemini 3.1 Ultra, its newest flagship AI model. Its headline feature is a **2-million-token context window** — roughly the amount of text in several long novels that the model can "hold in mind" at once when answering you.
- A token is just a chunk of text (about ¾ of a word). A bigger context window means the AI can read and reason over much more material in a single conversation without forgetting earlier parts.
- It is **natively multimodal**, meaning the same model handles text, images, audio, and video together rather than needing separate tools.
- A new sandboxed *Code Execution* tool lets the model write, run, and test code mid-conversation — useful for data tasks like the Python work you do.

**Why it matters:** Larger context windows let AI tackle whole documents, codebases, or datasets in one go, reducing the manual chopping-up that limits today's tools.

*Source: Crescendo AI News / LLM-Stats, June 2026*
