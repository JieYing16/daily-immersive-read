## 2026-06-15

### Topic: Geopolitics

**US and Iran Strike a Peace Deal**

- The United States and Iran announced they have reached an agreement to end their war, with both sides declaring an immediate and permanent halt to military operations on all fronts, including in Lebanon. An official signing ceremony is set for **Switzerland on Friday, June 19**.
- A headline term: the **Strait of Hormuz will reopen** once the deal is signed. This narrow waterway between Iran and Oman is one of the world's most important oil routes — roughly a fifth of global oil passes through it — so its closure during the war squeezed energy supplies.
- The path here was bumpy. Earlier in June, Iran briefly **suspended talks** in protest over Israel's actions in Lebanon before negotiations got back on track.
- One sticking point remains: Iran's nuclear program. Tehran says 60-day nuclear talks will only begin once the US **releases billions in frozen Iranian funds** — a claim a US official rejected.

**Why it matters:** Reopening the Strait of Hormuz could ease global oil prices and inflation, while the unresolved nuclear question signals the peace is fragile and far from final.

## 2026-06-14

### Topic: AI Technology

**Gemini 3.1 Ultra Stretches AI's Memory**

- Google released Gemini 3.1 Ultra, its newest flagship AI model. Its headline feature is a **2-million-token context window** — roughly the amount of text in several long novels that the model can "hold in mind" at once when answering you.
- A token is just a chunk of text (about ¾ of a word). A bigger context window means the AI can read and reason over much more material in a single conversation without forgetting earlier parts.
- It is **natively multimodal**, meaning the same model handles text, images, audio, and video together rather than needing separate tools.
- A new sandboxed *Code Execution* tool lets the model write, run, and test code mid-conversation — useful for data tasks like the Python work you do.

**Why it matters:** Larger context windows let AI tackle whole documents, codebases, or datasets in one go, reducing the manual chopping-up that limits today's tools.

*Source: Crescendo AI News / LLM-Stats, June 2026*
